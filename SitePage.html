Specific UX decisions

One of the first UX decisions we made was to decide on a color scheme. We used Paletton to come up with a viable scheme that we thought represented the idea of sustainability. The decision to choose a color scheme affects the accessibility of the site along with the desirability of the site as well. Additional time was also spend creating a navigation bar that would make navigating between the various sites easy. The navigation consideration was primarily dealing with the UX principles of findable and usable.

Difficulties

Over the course of the project we did run into a few difficulties with certain things. One of the major difficulties we encountered was getting the slide show to display pictures properly and to not have them weirdly rotated. Another difficulty we had was deciding on a good looking/appropriate color scheme. The final thing we had difficulty with was getting the textbox to pop up on the form.

Thoughts on sustainability

Our thoughts on sustainability are mixed. We believe that sustainability is a philosophy that needs to be embraced by people all over the planet, but at the same time we are worried that it might just be the current trendy “buzzword”. Only to be brushed aside and ignored when the next trendy idea or philosophy comes along. While this is one of our legitimate concerns with the philosophy of sustainability we do hope that this philosophy does become a permanent fixture around the world. Our opinion did not really change over the course of the project, but we did gain a greater awareness for sustainability efforts that our happening around JMU. Prior to this project we were aware that SSC was environmentally friendly but we were not aware that it was required to accomplish so many feats of sustainability in order to receive the LEEDS Gold certification. We thoroughly enjoyed getting to learn about the special features and processes like SSC’s waste mitigation process that enable the building to receive the Gold certification.
